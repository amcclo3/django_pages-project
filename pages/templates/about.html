An Intersting Fact About Me

On May 11, 2012, I was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes and conjestive heart failure. At the time of diagnosis, I was in what is called "Diabetic Ketoacidosis". In lamens terms, I was in a diabetes induced coma. Doctors were certain I wouldn't make it out of it after discovering the heart condition during my comatose state. I spent 6 days on life support. Towards the end, my doctors and family members were losing hope. However, by the grace of whatever higher power is out there, I awoke from my slumber (as I like to call it). For quite some time after being released from the hospital, I resented the world and my body for what it had done to me.

Today, I realize that this was a tool given me. It is a tool that I am able to utilize to help others who are going through what I did 8 years ago. The experience helped me to realize that I could make something positive, something GREAT out of a negative situation. I can now honestly say, that experience is one of the best things to ever happen to me. It has shaped me into the man I am today.
